Mission ¬∂

We advance the science of reflective alignment and reasoning stability.

We develop governance-grade diagnostics to evaluate whether advanced AI systems remain coherent under high-complexity conditions. Our work treats alignment not as an abstract intention, but as an internal property that can be examined, stressed, and verified.

  • - Test whether theoretical alignment guarantees survive real model behavior
  • - Provide auditable instruments for public-sector and institutional review
  • - Identify failure modes where reasoning degrades under scale or recursion

What We Evaluate ¬∂

We evaluate alignment as a dynamic property of reasoning systems, not as a static claim or stated objective.

Our work focuses on how alignment behaves under pressure: recursive self-reference, increasing model scale, and prolonged reasoning trajectories. These conditions are where failures emerge and where evaluation is most often absent.

To make this evaluation possible, we design and maintain a controlled alignment lexicon.

The lexicon constrains how core concepts are defined, referenced, and tested, eliminating semantic drift between theory, diagnostics, and interpretation. This allows us to:

  • - Evaluate reflective dynamics using stable, invariant definitions
  • - Detect reasoning drift that would otherwise be obscured by ambiguous language
  • - Compare alignment behavior across models, runs, and conditions without reinterpretation

The lexicon is not descriptive documentation. It is a measurement instrument.

Instruments

We develop reflective diagnostics to test how alignment behaves over time, not just how it is expressed at a single point.

These diagnostics subject reasoning systems to controlled stress: recursive self-reference, extended deliberation, and conditions where internal objectives must be preserved across transformation. The goal is not to elicit correct answers, but to observe whether alignment invariants remain intact as reasoning unfolds.

The diagnostics are constructed to operate directly over the controlled alignment lexicon, ensuring that detected changes reflect genuine reasoning drift rather than shifts in interpretation.

In practice, this enables us to:

  • - Stress-test reflective dynamics across prolonged reasoning trajectories
  • - Detect invariant violations before they manifest as surface-level errors
  • - Produce audit-ready evidence of reasoning stability or failure

The Reflective Alignment Lexicon and associated diagnostics are developed and maintained as a unified evaluation system. These instruments are designed to expose failure modes. Passing is not assumed.
