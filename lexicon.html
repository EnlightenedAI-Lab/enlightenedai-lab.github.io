01 //Overview

The Reflective Alignment Lexicon constrains how alignment-critical concepts are defined, referenced, and evaluated.

It exists to eliminate semantic drift between theory, diagnostics, and interpretation.

This lexicon is not descriptive documentation.

It is a measurement instrument.

02 //What the Lexicon Does

Advanced AI systems are evaluated using language.

If that language is unstable, the evaluation is invalid.

The lexicon fixes the meaning of core terms so that:

  • diagnostic results are interpretable,
  • comparisons across runs remain valid,
  • and observed changes correspond to real system behavior rather than reinterpretation.

Terms defined here may not be redefined elsewhere without explicit versioning.

03 //Core Terms

Each core term specifies:

  • a bounded semantic scope,
  • an operational interpretation,
  • and the conditions under which it may be considered violated.

These terms are intentionally narrow.

Breadth increases ambiguity; ambiguity invalidates measurement.

04 //Invariants

Invariants are properties that must hold across transformations of the system state.

Violation of an invariant constitutes a diagnostic failure, not an anomaly.

Invariant failure indicates:

  • reasoning instability,
  • semantic mutation,
  • or internal contradiction.

Passing is not assumed.

05 //Drift and Reinterpretation

Drift is defined as measurable deviation from invariant constraints without external prompting.

The lexicon prevents reinterpretation by binding terms to explicit constraints rather than narrative intent.

Without a lexicon, identical outputs may be read as compliant or non-compliant depending on observer bias.

With a lexicon, interpretation is constrained.

06 //Status and Versioning

The lexicon is a living artifact.

  • Definitions are versioned.
  • Revisions are logged.
  • Changes are auditable.

Stability of definitions is treated as a prerequisite for stability of conclusions.
